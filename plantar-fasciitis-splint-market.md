<p><p>The plantar fasciitis splint market consists primarily of two types: dorsal and traditional splints. Dorsal splints are designed to hold the foot in a neutral position, providing targeted support to the arch and alleviating tension on the plantar fascia, while allowing for more comfort and mobility. Traditional splints, on the other hand, tend to encompass a larger part of the foot and ankle, offering more rigid immobilization, which is beneficial for severe cases requiring extensive support during recovery.</p></p>
