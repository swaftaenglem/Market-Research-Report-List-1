<p><p>The battery-grade graphite market is primarily divided into two types: natural graphite and artificial graphite. Natural graphite is sourced from mining operations and is known for its high purity and excellent conductivity, making it suitable for lithium-ion batteries. In contrast, artificial graphite is synthetically produced, often from petroleum coke, and is tailored for specific applications, offering consistent quality and enhanced performance. Both types play crucial roles in meeting the demand for efficient energy storage solutions in the growing electric vehicle and renewable energy sectors.</p></p>
