<p><p>The quadrivalent flu vaccine market encompasses three main types of administration: intramuscular injection, nasal spray, and intradermal shot. Intramuscular injections are the most common method, delivering the vaccine deep into muscle tissue. Nasal sprays provide a needle-free option, utilizing a live attenuated vaccine for those aged 2-49. Intradermal shots are administered into the skin, offering a smaller dose with a potentially improved immune response. These diverse delivery methods cater to varying patient preferences and age groups, enhancing vaccination efforts.</p></p>
