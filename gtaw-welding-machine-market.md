<p><p>GTAW (Gas Tungsten Arc Welding), also known as TIG welding, is categorized mainly into two types based on the current used: AC (Alternating Current) and DC (Direct Current). AC is primarily used for aluminum and magnesium due to its ability to clean the oxide layer during the welding process, while DC provides a more stable arc for welding materials like steel and nickel. The choice between AC and DC depends on the specific materials being welded and the desired weld characteristics.</p></p>
