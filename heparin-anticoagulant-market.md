<p><p>The heparin anticoagulant market consists of three main types: Unfractionated Heparin (UF), Low Molecular Weight Heparin (LMWH), and Ultra-low Molecular Weight Heparin (ULMWH). UF is typically administered intravenously and is used for rapid anticoagulation. LMWH offers more predictable pharmacokinetics and is often preferred for outpatient settings. ULMWH is a newer category providing enhanced safety and efficacy, with a focus on minimizing side effects and improving patient outcomes. Each type caters to different clinical needs in anticoagulation therapy.</p></p>
